Back on Track: Modeling Chicago's Underfunded Pension System

MTFC 2025–26 Finalist · Team #23834 · Top 15 nationally out of 135 semi-finalists

Built three mathematical models to analyze whether Chicago's MEABF pension fund ($5.06B assets vs. $19.92B liabilities — a 25.4% funded ratio) can recover to the state-mandated 90% target by 2055.

Model 1 — Liability Projection: Exponential regression on 24 years of Public Plans Database data, adjusted for Illinois Tier 2 benefit transition with a piecewise growth function declining from 3.58% to 2.50% as Tier 2 workers replace retirees.

Model 2 — Monte Carlo Asset Simulation: 10,000 simulations across 5 investment strategies with correlated returns (Cholesky decomposition), stochastic contribution compliance modeled as a function of prior-year market performance, and full cash flow dynamics.

Model 3 — Annuity Valuation: Three-cohort insurance buyout model using 4.5% Moody's Aaa discount rate and 12% insurance markup to estimate $19.12B full buyout cost.

Key finding: Contribution compliance matters 3× more than investment strategy — a 79 percentage-point swing in funded ratio vs. 25pp from portfolio choice. With 100% compliance, every strategy achieves median funded ratio above 90%.

The Rise of Online Gambling: What's at Stake?

MathWorks M3 Challenge 2026 · Team #18486

Built three interconnected models analyzing the societal impact of online sports betting's expansion since the 2018 Supreme Court ruling (Murphy v. NCAA).

Q1 — Disposable Income Model: Calculated after-tax, after-essentials income using BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey data. Applied power-law income elasticities via log-log regression (R² > 0.87) with regional cost-of-living multipliers for US and UK. Food elasticity = 0.33, housing = 0.16, transport = 0.74.

Q2 — Expected Gambling Loss: Modeled annual losses as function of disposable income × 5-level risk tolerance scale × bet-type-specific house edge. Calibrated to match $15B US industry revenue within 3% margin.

Q3 — 30-Year Stochastic Savings Simulation: 10,000-trajectory Monte Carlo showing high-risk gamblers end with median savings of −$15,000 (74% chance of debt) vs. non-gamblers at $217,000.

Key finding: High-risk gambling costs more than all other entertainment combined; 84% of high-risk gamblers lose >10% of disposable income annually. The problem is concentrated in young men 18–34.

Impact of Chess Rating on Performance Metrics

Inspirit AI · Best Social Impact Research Paper 2025

Analyzed 18,000+ Lichess games studying the relationship between player rating and centipawn loss across three tiers (800–1500, 1500–2000, 2000–3000). Trained a Random Forest Regressor achieving MAE of 238 rating points. Performed hyperparameter tuning on max_depth, max_leaf_nodes, and max_features — with max_depth producing the most significant improvement.

Key finding: No direct correlation between centipawn loss and online rating — external factors beyond move quality influence online rating, supporting the hypothesis that online ratings don't fully reflect true chess strength.
